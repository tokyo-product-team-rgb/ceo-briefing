The big story

AI's energy appetite triggers "Manhattan Project" response. US Energy Secretary Chris Wright compared AI's energy demands to a Manhattan Project-scale challenge. AI datacenters drove 42% rise in US electricity costs. Response: 5 GW of new natural gas plants (NRG, GE Vernova, Kiewit) for ERCOT and PJM, plus accelerated nuclear revival. States from Arizona to Wyoming exploring nuclear for AI power.

Key developments

AI Demand

AI datacenters overwhelm grid — unprecedented demand growth

Exelon CEO: "Data center growth overwhelming supply." Hyperscalers need 24/7 carbon-free power that solar alone can't provide. Microsoft, Google, Amazon signing nuclear PPAs. 58% of new nuclear agreements now going to AI datacenters.

Natural Gas

5 GW of new gas plants announced for ERCOT and PJM markets

NRG Energy, GE Vernova, and Kiewit partnering on new combined-cycle plants. Operations targeted for 2029-2032. Grid reliability concerns driving near-term fossil investment despite climate goals.

Nuclear

States explore nuclear for AI — Arizona, California, Wyoming leading

Federal emphasis on nuclear scalability for AI/tech. Japan accelerating restarts — 7 additional reactors approved for 2026, targeting 22% nuclear share by 2030. Takaichi citing industrial competitiveness.

Fusion

Fusion advances with AI-driven plasma design — Proxima and Commonwealth progressing

AI accelerating fusion development — plasma optimization now uses machine learning. Commonwealth Fusion $1.8B Series D imminent at $8B+ valuation. Fusion offers compact, always-on power without long-lived waste.

Solar

Solar LCOE hits $35/MWh — now competitive with US gas plants

Global solar LCOE down 2% in 2025, projected 31% lower by 2035. Inverted perovskite cells hit 25.6% efficiency. China dominates manufacturing; storage remains the bottleneck for grid reliability.

Battery

US battery storage sets record — 18.2 GW added in 2025

EIA reports unprecedented expansion via lithium-ion advances and incentives. LCOE falls 11% in 2025, 50% projected by 2035. Alternatives like vanadium flow and sodium batteries gaining interest for grid-scale.

Geothermal

AI accelerates geothermal — Zanskar's New Mexico plant advances

AI improving subsurface mapping and drilling efficiency. 24/7 baseload power without intermittency. Growing interest from hyperscalers seeking diverse carbon-free sources.

Bottom line

AI's energy appetite is reshaping the entire power sector. The "Manhattan Project" framing from Energy Secretary Wright captures the scale of the challenge — grid infrastructure can't keep pace with datacenter growth. Near-term response includes 5 GW of new gas plants, but the long-term bet is on nuclear renaissance and fusion development. Solar hits cost parity but storage remains the constraint. Japan leading on nuclear restarts while investing in energy security.

AI straining grid. Nuclear renaissance accelerating. Solar at parity. Storage critical.
