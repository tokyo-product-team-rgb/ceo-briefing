Tap to expand

What: Bank of Japan Governor Kazuo Ueda warned that escalating Middle East tensions could materially impact Japan's economy, signaling the central bank may keep policy rates unchanged for a prolonged period. Why this matters: This is a notable shift from the BOJ's hawkish posture earlier this year. Markets now pricing near-zero probability of a rate hike before July.
