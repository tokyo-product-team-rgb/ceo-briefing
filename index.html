Current status: Functionally blocked. AIS tracking data shows zero confirmed transits on March 14 — the first full day of no crossings. WAR RISK INSURANCE

Insurance Premiums Surge 300%+ — VLCCs Now Pay $2–3M Per Voyage

War risk insurance for Strait of Hormuz transits has surged to 1% of hull replacement value (renewable every 7 days), up from 0.25% pre-conflict. For a typical VLCC valued at $100M, this means $2–3 million per voyage, up from ~$250K. Medium Range tankers face additional war risk premiums of $40K–$120K for 7-day coverage (up 4x). Cargo insurance is up 150–200% for Gulf transits. The Joint War Committee expanded its war zone designation (JWLA-033) on March 5 to cover waters around Bahrain, Qatar, and Oman — voiding prior policies. VLCC charter rates have quadrupled to $538K–$800K/day.

NAVAL PRESENCE

No Active Escort Operations — US Navy "Prepared If Needed" But Not Currently Escorting

Despite the near-total shipping halt, no coalition naval escort operations are currently active in the strait. The US Navy has briefed the shipping and oil industry but stated it is "prepared if needed" rather than providing active escorts. US destroyers have been observed sailing through the strait (March 15 reports), but commercial escort convoys have not materialized. The International Transport Workers' Federation has designated the area as a High Risk Area, and crew safety concerns are the primary driver keeping tankers away — even more than insurance costs. Key gap: Without escorts, commercial shipping resumption depends entirely on de-escalation.
