Renesas Sells Timing Chip Business to US-Based SiTime for $3B ‚Äî Pivoting to Core Automotive MCUs

Renesas Electronics will divest its clocks and timing device business to American chip design company SiTime for approximately $3 billion. The sale was driven by Renesas posting a loss in the first 9 months of 2025, forcing management to shed non-core businesses and refocus on its strongest segment: microcontrollers (MCUs) used in automobiles and industrial equipment. The timing business was originally acquired as part of Renesas's $6.7B purchase of US-based Integrated Device Technology in 2019 ‚Äî this divestiture represents an acknowledgment that the IDT integration didn't deliver the expected synergies in all segments. For SiTime, the acquisition strengthens its position as a specialist in precision timing semiconductors, a niche but critical component in 5G, autonomous vehicles, and data centers.
